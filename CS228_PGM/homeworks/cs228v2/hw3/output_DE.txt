marginal probabilities depend significantly on the clique used to calculate them
(that is, the choice between all the cliques containing a specified variable).

More specifically, picking parity factors produces more errors, compared to
picking single-variable factors.

The plot "plot_d_many_errors.png" corresponds to using parity factors (just for
the sake of comparison).
The other plots correspond to using single-variable factors to maximize quality.
